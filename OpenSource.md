# Promoting A2AL as an Open Source Standard

## Step 1 – Formalize the Specification
- Publish the A2AL/2.0 specification
- Freeze the seven archetypes and message grammar
- Establish semantic versioning (MAJOR.MINOR.PATCH)

## Step 2 – Neutral Positioning
- Maintain vendor neutrality
- Do not bind the standard to a specific LLM, framework, or ecosystem
- Position A2AL as complementary to orchestration frameworks, not competitive

## Step 3 – Reference Materials
- Provide a minimal grammar/spec file
- Include worked examples for each archetype
- Publish a reference “Markdown → A2AL” transpilation example

## Step 4 – Early Adopters
Target communities with immediate pain:
- Enterprise AI orchestration teams
- Security and compliance groups
- Multi-agent research and ops teams
- Regulated industries with audit pressure

## Step 5 – Governance

- Establish a lightweight spec council
- Require backward compatibility guarantees
- Publish all change proposals publicly
- Avoid fast-breaking evolution

## Outcome
A2AL positions itself as the **POSIX-layer for agent communication** in execution-heavy, real-world environments.
